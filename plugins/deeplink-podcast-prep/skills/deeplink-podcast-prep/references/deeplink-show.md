# Deeplink Show — format & how prep is structured

Guest-agnostic reference for the **Deeplink Show** podcast. Use it to understand the show's shape
so the prep matches how the episode actually runs. The show is the constant; the guest and topics
change every episode.

## Show format (observed)

- **Language:** Czech.
- **Length:** long-form free discussion, ~75–90 min.
- **Nature:** the host's question list is a *backbone, not a script* — a real conversation, so prep
  should give the guest flexible talking points, not memorized answers.
- **Host doc:** the host sends the guest a Notion page ahead of time containing: a short framing of
  the episode's umbrella themes, a **host intro** paragraph (naming the guest, their role/company,
  and why they were invited), and **question blocks** with rough time budgets.

## Typical block arc

The host tailors blocks to each guest, but the arc is recognizable and prep should expect it:

1. **Block 0 — warm-up / who is the guest (~first 10 min).** Opens with a personal color story
   (an unusual hobby, trip, or fact the host found) + "what do you actually do" + the guest's path.
2. **Middle blocks — the guest's world.** What their company/field does, how it grew, concrete
   examples, the hard/technical substance. Usually 2–4 blocks, tuned to the guest's expertise.
3. **Philosophy block (~near the end).** Contrarian / big-picture questions: what's overhyped,
   who is this really for, what's changing in the field.
4. **Practical & outlook block (last, time permitting).** Actionable tips for the listener and a
   2–3 year forward look.

Prep should mirror this: keep block 0 light and personal, load the middle with concrete facts and
examples, give the philosophy block sharp opinions, and make the practical block genuinely useful
to a listener (often a smaller-business owner or practitioner).

## Illustrative input (fictional) — what the host's Notion page looks like

A short, made-up example (no real person) so you recognize the shape of the doc this skill consumes:
a framing line, a host intro that names the guest and their field, and question blocks tagged to the
generic topic buckets from `prep-method.md`.

**Umbrella themes:** what building X in the guest's field actually takes; where the hype outruns
reality; practical advice for the listener.

**Host intro (fictional):**
> Naším dnešním hostem je [jméno] — [role] ve [firma], [jedna věta, proč je zajímavý]. [Osobní háček,
> který si moderátor našel.]

**Question blocks (fictional):**

- **Blok 0 — Krátké představení (~do 10. min)** → `bio`
  - [Osobní/barvitá historka, kterou moderátor našel.] Co to bylo a co sis odnesl?
  - Pro lidi mimo obor: co vlastně děláš?
  - Tvoje cesta k téhle roli — jak se to semlelo?
- **Blok 1 — [Firma / obor hosta] (~do 25. min)** → `company`
  - Co [firma] dělá a jaký problém řeší? Jak je velká, jak vyrostla?
  - [Konkrétní milník — investice, akvizice, číslo — který otázka zmiňuje.]
  - Oblíbený příklad, kde to reálně změnilo byznys / život lidí?
- **Blok 2 — [Odborné jádro tématu] (~do 45. min)** → `domain-substance`
  - [Otázky na podstatu oboru, často s vloženým faktem nebo statistikou k ověření.]
- **Blok 3 — [Druhé odborné jádro / technologie] (~do 65. min)** → `domain-substance`
  - [Jak to technicky funguje? Rizika? Jak se řeší X?]
- **Blok 4 — Trocha filozofie (~do 80. min)** → `philosophy`
  - Co je přeceněné / kam se to cpe, kam nepatří? Pro koho to dává a nedává smysl?
  - Jak to promění práci lidí v oboru?
- **Blok 5 — Praktické tipy a výhled (dle času)** → `practical`
  - Pro posluchače (často menší firma / jednotlivec): první rozumný krok?
  - Jeden konkrétní tip / na co si dát pozor.
  - Kam to celé spěje za 2–3 roky?

Topic tags (`bio`, `company`, `domain-substance`, `philosophy`, `practical`) are the generic buckets
used in `prep-method.md`; the specific labels and number of middle blocks differ per guest, but the
arc maps cleanly.
